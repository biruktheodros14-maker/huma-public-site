Overview

Why HUMA matters

HUMA is designed to support individualized physiology modeling rather than relying only on broad population averages. The goal is a modular framework that can represent a specific person's biological context, simulate intervention effects, and support better clinical reasoning.

The work combines computational modeling, modular architecture, validation experiments, and a human-centered application layer that can communicate results to researchers, clinicians, and other stakeholders.

Architecture

Five layers of individualized modeling

1

Structural

Organ geometry, compartments, and physiological layout.

2

Functional

Flows, clearances, transport, and dynamic process behavior.

3

Molecular

Ions, storage, signaling, transporters, and biochemical state.

4

Regulatory

Hormonal and feedback systems that drive adaptation over time.

5

Contextual

Age, environment, ancestry, diet, genetics, and lived conditions.

HUMA is an independent effort focused on building more individualized, interpretable physiological modeling systems and turning that technical work into something people can actually use.

The project sits at the intersection of computational modeling, health systems thinking, and human-centered product design. That combination matters because the goal is not just to simulate biology, but to make those simulations useful for real decision-making.

If you are interested in the architecture, validation direction, or the long-term vision for individualized digital twins, this site is meant to be a starting point for that conversation.
